by Lung Ka Ho, Jack

"Ding! Ding! Ding!" Continuous news push notifications pop up on the screen of a cell phone. The push notifications are the window allowing people to grasp the world. Meanwhile, this "world window" has a love-hate relationship with its user.

According to AIRSHIP, a software for creating push notifications, A push notification is a message that pops up on a mobile device. App publishers can send them at any time; users do not have to be in the app or using their devices to receive them. Push notification is widely used on different news apps to send users the latest news.

Are news push notifications being over used?

Over 50% of respondents agree that news apps are sending too many notification

Nearly every mainstream news outlets are using the push notification function nowadays. Based on this phenomenon, a survey was undertaken to know the receivers' thought. A 4-weeks data set of the 6 most installed news apps in this survey was collected to find out the habit of each news app on sending push notifications as well. All 1862 pushes were collected and put into a single category with totally 14 categories.

The survey shows that the number of push notifications sent by the news apps is a bit over. Over half of the respondents agree that these apps are sending too much push notification.

News push notifications have been used

According to the survey, respondents have enabled push notifications function from about 2 news apps on average. It means that they are averaging receiving about 23 pushes per day only by news apps. When respondents were asked for a fair number of push notifications should be sent by a single app per day. 173/233(74.25%) of respondents think that push notifications of a single app needed to be sent within 10 times per day only. However, based on the push notifications for the top 6 most frequent used apps in this survey. They were sending averaging 11.6 times per day.

They are just sending too much, it feel like there are hundreds news notifications spamming to my phone.

A random respondent
Apple daily is the most installed app in this survey (72.1% of respondents have installed). They were even sending 13.39 times per day. It shows that the number of sending push notifications is slightly more than respondents' expectations. Comparing with all 6 most installed news app, On.cc has sent 494 push notifications in these 4 weeks, averaging about 17 pushes per day. On.cc was sending the most push notifications within these 6 apps. HK01 was the less, they just sent 115 push notifications, averaging about 4 of them per day.

News push notifications content VS respondent's preferences

Based on the survey result, the content of push notifications also did not fit their expectations.

When compare the pushing frequency of different categories with the respondent's preferences. Covid19 News and Daily News are the top 2 ranks in both pushing frequency, and respondent's preferences, which means that the respondents are very willing to receive push notifications of Covid19 News and Daily News. They are averagely the most sent notifications within these 6 news apps as well.

Over half of the respondents agree that only breaking news notifications should be pushed.

I only read breaking news push notifications. All news push notifications are useless apart from breaking news push notifications.

It points out that the preferences in a different group of people on receiving push notifications are actually different in some circumstances. But most of the apps from the mainstream news outlets do not provide a filter function for their users to choose what kinds of news push notification they would like to receive. Now News is the only app that provides this function within the 6 most installed news app in this survey.

I think that the news push notifications are annoying. Apart from reading the breaking news, I don't think they are useful. I actually installed a lot of news app on my phone, however I turn off the push notifications function for most of them.

Miss Ip, Creative Manager in Next Media

When it comes to the turnoff rate of push notification function on news apps, the average turnoff rate is 33.79%. Over one-third of news apps have been turned off on the push notification function. Nearly half of the respondent’s (45.92%) turned off at least one app.


News push notifications are actually useful in some circumstances

Although the frequency of sending push notification failed to meet the respondents' expectation, sometimes, most of the respondents still find them helpful. According to the survey result. 78% of them agree that news push notification is actually useful. Most of them think that it can remind them of the news that was missed and help them know what is happening without reading the whole news article. Nearly all of them (93%) agree that news push notifications can let them catch up with the latest news.

News push notifications can......

However, not all of the respondents find push notification useful. The survey result shows that respondents with a lower frequency of reading news are lesser satisfied with news push notifications. The lower the frequency of reading news, the more they feel push notifications are annoying and have repeated content.
When analyzing the result by education level, it shows that respondents with higher education level find push notifications more useful to help them catch up with the latest news.

Fear of missing out on news push notifications

Fear of missing out (FOMO) is one reason why respondents still turn on push notification despite the problems mentioned earlier. FOMO is anxiety over missing out or being out of touch with events, experiences, or interactions. It often aroused by posts seen on social media. But it is found that this phenomenon affects the respondent's habits on receiving news notification in this survey as well.

Respondents were asked 3 hypophysis questions to know the anxiety when "missed" some news. They are asked how anxious you would feel if you cannot receive any push notification from news apps / social media and how anxious would you feel if everyone is talking about a piece of news that you do not know.

The average anxious level of inability to receive any push notification from news apps / social media is very close. They are 4.146 and 4.691 with a scale of 10 (higher values = more anxious). The survey result also shows a position relationship (R=0.61) between these two sets of data. It means that respondents who feel more anxious when not able to receive push notifications from social media would feel more anxious when able to receive push notifications from news apps as well.

Applying FOMO into news push notification, the survey result shows that respondents who have a higher anxious level when not receiving push notifications from news apps would have a lower rate of turning news push notifications. In other words, respondents who have more anxiety of missing a news push notification would allow more news apps to send them the notifications.

Based on the article was published in Computers and Human Behavior and named as Motivational, emotional. Behavioural correlates of Fear of missing out, Fear of missing out was found to be associated with a lower sense of having one's needs met as well as a lower feeling of life satisfaction in general.

However, when it comes to FOMO on the news, it was found several factors that can affect the anxiety level when "missing the news."

According to Aarti Gupta, a psychologist from Anxiety & Depression Association of America, admitting you have a problem is your first step to tackle FOMO. "You're acknowledging the insecurity, and with that recognition, you can now tackle the problem."

She also suggested time management on the phone is a crucial step to overcome FOMO. People could set a specific time to check the news notification.

According to the Verywell Mind website, changing the focus is one of the methods to overcome FOMO. "You can change your feed to show you less of what triggers your FOMO and more of what makes you feel good about yourself. Work on identifying what may be sapping your joy online. Work to minimize these as you add more to your feed (and life) that makes you happy."

Moreover, seeking help from reliable people is a way to release the stress from FOMO. FOMO is common in society, which means some people understand your feelings. Everyone feels a certain level of FOMO at different times in their lives. You can talk to them to soothe the anxiety from FOMO.
